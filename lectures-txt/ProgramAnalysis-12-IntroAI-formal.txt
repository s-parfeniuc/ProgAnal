===== slide 4 =====
What went wrong?
The previous argument is obviously just a joke and our proof cheats
"Interesting" is neither a formal nor a semantic property, it depends on the
enumeration, it is self-referential, it is not a property of program behaviour
So what kind of properties do we actually care about?
Semantic properties depends on the input-output behaviour of the program
A property is non-trivial if: it holds for some program and not for others
RiceвҖҷs Theorem: Every non-trivial semantic property of programs is undecidable.

===== slide 5 =====
Yes, No, Don't know
Due to Rice's Theorem, all semantic properties P(c) of programs are undecidable,
thus we cannot design fully precise static analysers able to automatically infer P(c)
Nevertheless, we aim to ensure the correctness (soundness) of static analysis:

вҖў If the analysis outputs YES, then the P(c) is certainly verified
вҖў If the analysis does not output YES then it may happen that P(c) is not verified
"not YES" means "Don't know"
